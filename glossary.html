Aracha

Aracha is a very basic tea. It basically contains all the parts of the Camellia Sinensis, that can be used to create Tea. So this tea consists of: Leaves, Leaf Stalk, Stems, Leaf Hair as well as broken Leaves. All these ingredients are steamed and rolled.

Usually Aracha is beeing finished after it has been sold, which means that Aracha is mainy as basis for other Green Teas to build upon. Aracha is for example used to blend different Flavorprofiles of Sencha.

Gyokuro, »Jewel Dew«

Gyokuro is one of most high quality Teas Japan has to offer. Of course also within this type of Tea there are different stages of quality. This type of Tea grows for 21 days in the shade before beeing harvested. Gyokuro is grown on a special variety of the Camellia Sinensis, such as Asahi, Okumidori, Yamakai, and Saemidori. This Tea is in need of special care, when prepared. The name »Jewel Dew« refers to the pale green color of the Tea.

Tea Leaves~ 6-10 g / 180 ml
Temperature~ 50-60 °C
Brewing Time~ 01:30 Minutes
Infusions~ 3-4

The highest quality Gyokuros can be infused with a water temperature of ~ 40 °C. In some cases even the Ice-Methode can be used.

BOH

»BOH« or »BOH Plantations Sdn Bhd« is a Malaysian tea brand specializing in Black Tea. »BOH« is one of the largest manufacturers of Black Tea in the World. The »BOH Tea Plantation« is located in the Cameron Highlands in Malaysia.

[The »BOH Tea Plantation« can also be visited. A visit offers rare insights into the processing of Black Tea as well as Tea-Shopping directly on the Plantation itself.]

TWG Tea

»TWG Tea« is a Singaporean tea brand as well as teahouse chain, that offers luxurious tea variations and experiences.

[»TWG« derives from the brands self-conception as »Tea Wellness Group«; a brand specializing in teas for Luxury and Lifestyle.]

Kukicha

Kukicha is a japenese Green Tea, which mainly consists of Leaf Veins and Stems, and therefore also contains less theine. The quality of Kukicha is similar to the quality of Sencha.

If Kukicha consist of Leaf Vains that were during the preparation of Gyokuro, it is called Karigane or Shiarore to indicate the special taste.

Tea Leaves~ 3-4 g / 180 ml
Temperature~ 90 °C

Kamairicha

Kamairicha is very different from other Japanese Teas. In fact this Tea doesn't even fit the typical flavor profile of Japenese Green Tea. Very early in the process of creating this Tea, the Leaves are roasted at about ~ 300 °C. These Leaves are not steamed. Typically this Tea is shaped into comma or Magatama. The shape of the processed Tea Leaves reminds more of Chinese Teas than Japanese.

This Tea is said have a slightly roasted flavor and fresh sweetness.

Tea Leaves~ 5 g / 450 ml
Temperature~ 80 °C
Brewing Time~ 01:00 Minutes
Infusions~ 2
